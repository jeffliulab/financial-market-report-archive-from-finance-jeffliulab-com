# Daily Intelligence Briefing - April 22, 2026

> ***Markets price higher-for-longer rates and elevated geopolitical risk, fueling a rotation from bond proxies into commodities and selective tech names.***

---

## 🌍 Global Headlines

**Geopolitical Tensions Escalate** – Renewed hostilities in the Middle East and stalled ceasefire talks between Russia and Ukraine have pushed gold above $4,780 and crude near $90. European defense stocks saw inflows as NATO reaffirmed supply commitments, while safe-haven demand underpinned the Swiss franc and Japanese yen.

**ECB Signals Caution** – ECB President Lagarde reiterated that rate cuts remain data-dependent, pushing eurozone bond yields higher. Markets now price the first cut no earlier than September, weighing on rate-sensitive sectors in Europe.

**Japan Inflation Data Due** – Tokyo CPI expected to hold above 3%, reinforcing expectations for a BoJ rate hike in June. The yen strengthened slightly against the dollar, while the Nikkei edged up 0.4% on tech export optimism.

---

## 🇺🇸 United States

**Fed Speak Drives Rate Repricing** – Several Fed officials, including Governor Waller and Chicago President Goolsbee, struck a hawkish tone, emphasizing that inflation progress has stalled. Markets responded by lifting 5-year yields 1.5% to 3.91% and the 10-year to 4.29%, as expectations for a May rate cut faded entirely. The probability of a June cut slipped to 45%, down from 60% a week ago.

**Housing Data Mixed** – March existing home sales fell 2.8% month-over-month, below consensus, as mortgage rates hover near 7%. New home starts, however, surprised to the upside, driven by multifamily construction in the South.

**Earnings Season Underway** – With 20% of S&P 500 companies having reported, beats are running at 78% but guidance has been cautious. Microsoft’s strong cloud numbers lifted the stock 1.46%, while Apple slumped 2.52% on reports of iPhone supply chain delays in Southeast Asia.

---

## 🇨🇳 China & Asia-Pacific

**China Stimulus Hopes Lift Markets** – The SSE Composite rose 0.52% on speculation that Beijing will unveil a new round of fiscal stimulus at the upcoming Politburo meeting. Rumors of a cut in the reserve requirement ratio (RRR) and increased infrastructure spending drove gains in property and materials stocks. The yuan held steady at 6.82 per dollar as the PBOC continued its managed depreciation.

**Taiwan Semiconductor Under Pressure** – TSMC shares fell 1.2% in Taipei amid reports that the U.S. may expand export controls on advanced chip equipment. The stock is down 8% year-to-date on geopolitical jitters.

**Japan’s Nikkei Gains on Weak Yen** – The yen’s modest weakness (USD/JPY above 151) boosted exporter stocks, with Toyota and Sony each up over 1%. The Nikkei 225 closed at 59,586, just shy of its all-time high.

The real story is the severe underperformance of rate-sensitive sectors: Utilities and Real Estate each fell more than 1.7% as Treasury yields climbed sharply. The rotation tells us that while the VIX is low, sentiment is not uniformly risk-on. The defensive sectors that typically shine in a risk-off environment (utilities, staples, real estate) actually lagged, indicating that the selloff is more about rate repricing than broad fear. Energy's strength may reflect a "dollar down, commodity up" trade as the USD weakened.

**Apple Supply Chain Woes** – Shares fell sharply after reports that component shortages in Vietnam and India could delay iPhone 18 production by 2–4 weeks. The company also faces potential tariff exposure on Chinese-made devices.

**Goldman Sachs Revenue Miss** – Investment banking fees fell 12% quarter-over-quarter, dragging the stock 1.6% lower. The firm cited a slowdown in M&A due to geopolitical uncertainty.

**Crypto Resurgence** – Bitcoin’s rally above $78,000 marks its highest level in three weeks, driven by institutional inflows and a weakening dollar. Open interest in BTC futures rose 8% on the day.
